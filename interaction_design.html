Interaction Design Coursework — Energy Garden

View full report ↗

A Spring 2024 studio project exploring how playful feedback loops can reshape everyday energy habits. Research uncovered behavioural frictions and inspired a mobile app that rewards sustainable streaks while keeping comfort and control in the homeowner’s hands.

Research Signals

Semi-structured interviews with renters, families, and eco-conscious homeowners revealed that automation alone rarely changes behaviour. We clustered feedback into motivation pillars that guided ideation.

Convenience

Participants resisted tools that added chores; our interface had to fit inside existing routines.

Comfort

People were happy to save energy so long as warmth, light, and personal downtime stayed intact.

Cost & Impact

Real-time bill estimates and environmental progress bars made sustainable actions feel tangible.

Design Evolution

Affinity Mapping

Clustered interview quotes into convenience, comfort, cost, scheduling, cognition, and planetary care hubs to define our challenge.

Concept Sprints

Sketched three divergent concepts—an energy-saving forest game, smart chargers, and an adaptive notification coach.

Energy Garden Selection

Chose the gamified concept for its ability to keep users in control while nudging behaviour through streaks and micro-rewards.

Prototype Loops

Iterated Figma wireframes, motion studies, and storyboards to visualise streak resets, forest growth, and smart-meter integrations.

Motivational System Architecture

Energy Garden connects household meters to playful dashboards. The system balances short-term wins with long-term ecological impact, reinforcing routines without punitive automation.

Daily Streaks

Players earn daily streaks by staying under personalised consumption thresholds, fuelling intrinsic motivation.

Forest Health

Each saved kilowatt feeds a virtual forest, providing a calming, ambient reminder of progress.

Battery Bank

Spare savings accumulate into a “power bank” economy that unlocks custom forest assets and peer challenges.
